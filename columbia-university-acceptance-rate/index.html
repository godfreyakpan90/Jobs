Columbia University employs a highly competitive admissions process, featuring a low acceptance rate. Nevertheless, Columbia follows a holistic approach in its admissions procedure, considering factors beyond just grades and test scores. A compelling application essay, impressive letters of recommendation, notable involvement in extracurricular activities, and completion of a rigorous academic curriculum can all contribute to a successful application.

Columbia University Acceptance Rate 2026

In regards to admission, Columbia University’s overall acceptance rate is 7%, with a 3.73% acceptance rate for regular decision and a 10.31% acceptance rate for early decision. The university received 60,377 applications for the Class of 2026. The acceptance rate for the Class of 2026 was 3.73%, which was nearly equivalent to the previous year’s rate of 3.66% for the Class of 2025.

Gaining admission to Columbia University is extremely challenging, particularly due to the university’s exceptionally low acceptance rate, which is among the lowest in the world. In the regular decision cycle, Columbia received 54,072 applications, with only 1,603 applicants being accepted. As one of the most rigorous Ivy League institutions, Columbia accepted just 2.96% of applicants during the regular admissions process.

Columbia Admission Rate 2023

The Columbia admission rate 2023 is 3.7%. This means that, out of the total number of students in 100% that applied for admission in the 2023 year, 3.7% will be accepted. The acceptance can be due to lots of factors.

Columbia University’s graduate school has an overall acceptance rate of 5.1%. To gain admission to Columbia’s graduate programs, applicants need to have an undergraduate GPA of at least 3.0. The university’s Graduate Admissions Committee looks for candidates who have displayed research and writing skills.

Columbia University Transfer Acceptance Rate

Columbia University’s transfer acceptance rate is higher than the first-year acceptance rate, standing at 14.7% for the 2022-2023 cycle. This is due to the transfer applicant pool being smaller than the first-year pool. If the first-year acceptance rate doesn’t work out for you, consider attending another institution and applying as a transfer student.

Apart from the acceptance rate, it’s important to consider financial aid at Columbia University. In the class of 2026, more than half of admitted students received need-based financial aid. Columbia also pledges to meet 100% of demonstrated need for all first-year students, making it a great financial option for families.

Get Started with Columbia Admissions Process

The steps to complete the Columbia University admissions process may vary depending on the program you are applying to. However, in general, here are some steps you may need to follow:
  1. Create an account on the Columbia Admissions Portal.
  2. Choose the program you want to apply for and fill out the application form.
  3. Submit official transcripts from all previously attended institutions.
  4. Submit test scores, such as the SAT or ACT for undergraduate programs, or GRE or GMAT for graduate programs.
  5. Submit essays or personal statements.
  6. Submit letters of recommendation from teachers, counselors, or other academic or professional references.
  7. Pay the application fee.
  8. Check your application status regularly on the Columbia Admissions Portal.

Columbia University Application Fee

As of the 2022-2023 academic year, the application fee for Columbia University is $85 USD for both undergraduate and graduate programs.
