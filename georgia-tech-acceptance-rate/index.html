Georgia Tech Admissions

The Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) has a holistic admissions process that takes into account a range of factors beyond just grades and test scores. Some of the key factors that the admissions committee considers include academic performance, extracurricular activities, essays, letters of recommendation, and other achievements. Georgia Tech offers undergraduate, graduate, and professional degree programs in a variety of fields, including engineering, computing, business, sciences, design, and liberal arts. To apply for admission, interested students should visit the Georgia Tech admissions website and follow the application instructions for their desired program of study.

How hard is it to Get into Georgia Tech

Getting into the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) is very challenging, as the university is highly selective and seeks to admit students who demonstrate academic excellence, intellectual curiosity, leadership potential, and a commitment to community service. The acceptance rate for incoming freshmen at Georgia Tech for the 2022-2023 academic year was 17%, indicating that it is difficult to gain admission to the university.

In addition to high academic achievement, Georgia Tech also looks for applicants who demonstrate a strong commitment to their chosen field of study and have demonstrated leadership, innovation, and creativity. Meeting or exceeding the minimum requirements for admission does not guarantee acceptance, as the admissions process is holistic and considers many factors beyond just academic performance, such as extracurricular activities, essays, and letters of recommendation. Overall, admission to Georgia Tech is highly competitive, and prospective applicants should be prepared to present a well-rounded and competitive application.

Georgia Tech Average GPA

The average GPA for admitted students at the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) for the 2023-2024 academic year was 4.11 on a 4.0 scale.

Georgia Tech Early Action

Georgia Tech offers an Early Action (EA) application option for freshman applicants. The EA application deadline for the 2022-2023 academic year was October 15, 2022, and decisions were released by January 15, 2023. Students who apply through the EA option receive an admission decision earlier than those who apply through Regular Decision. However, applying through EA is not a binding agreement to attend Georgia Tech if accepted, and students are still free to apply to other colleges and universities until the Regular Decision deadline.

Georgia Tech Transfer Acceptance Rate

The transfer acceptance rate at the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) varies depending on the specific program of study and the applicant pool. For the 2023-2024 academic year, the overall transfer acceptance rate at Georgia Tech was 23.1%. Out of 7,578 transfer applicants, 1,752 were admitted.

Georgia Tech Application Deadline

The application deadline for the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) varies depending on the program of study. For freshman applicants, the Regular Decision application deadline for the 2023-2024 academic year is January 1, 2023. Early Action application deadline for the 2023-2024 academic year has already passed, which was October 15, 2022. For transfer applicants, the application deadline for the Fall 2023 semester was March 1, 2023, and for the Spring 2024 semester, the application deadline is October 1, 2023.

Georgia Tech out of State Acceptance Rate

The Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) does not differentiate admission rates based on residency status. The overall acceptance rate for the 2023-2024 academic year was 17%, meaning that out-of-state applicants had the same chance of admission as in-state applicants.

Georgia Tech Tuition

For the 2022-2023 academic year, the tuition and fees for undergraduate students at the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) are:

  • In-State Tuition and Fees: $12,682 per semester or $25,364 per year
  • Out-of-State Tuition and Fees: $33,794 per semester or $67,588 per year
