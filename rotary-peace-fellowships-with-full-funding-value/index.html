Rotary Peace Fellowships with Full Funding Value

The Rotary Peace Fellowships are currently open, presenting an exciting opportunity for individuals interested in pursuing a master’s degree or professional development certificate in the fields of peace and conflict resolution. This article aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the scholarship program, including its benefits and a step-by-step guide to the application process. If you’re passionate about making a positive impact on global peace and conflict issues, the Rotary Peace Fellowships could be the ideal path to further education and a meaningful career in this field.

The Rotary Peace Fellowships offer fully funded scholarship opportunities for international students. Specifically tailored for those pursuing master’s degree programs in peace and conflict resolution, these scholarships provide comprehensive coverage, encompassing tuition and fees, room and board, round-trip transportation, as well as all expenses related to internships and field studies. This generous financial support empowers students to engage in advanced studies and practical experiences that contribute to peacebuilding and conflict resolution efforts globally.

Given the high level of competitiveness, the Rotary Peace Fellowships are awarded to a limited number of students each year, with up to 150 fellowships granted annually. These fellowships are designed for individuals pursuing either a Professional Development Certificate Program or a Master’s Degree Program with a focus on peace, conflict resolution, and prevention. Since its establishment in 2002, the Rotary Peace Centers have successfully trained over 1,400 fellows who now hold significant roles in various sectors and organizations across more than 115 countries. These esteemed alumni serve as leaders in governmental agencies, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), the military, education, law enforcement, and international institutions such as the United Nations and the World Bank. Through their exceptional contributions, they help promote peace and conflict resolution efforts on a global scale.

Scholarship Coverage

Rotary Peace Fellowships covers

  • Tuition and fees,
  • Room and board,
  • Round-trip transportation,
  • All internship and field-study expenses

Eligibility Criteria for Rotary Peace Fellowships

Rotary Peace Fellowships are specifically designed for professionals who possess work experience in the field of international relations or in peace and conflict prevention and resolution. Applicants are required to meet the following criteria:

  • Eligible Countries: All countries around the world
  • Proficiency in English and a strong command of a second language are highly recommended
  • Exceptional leadership skills
  • Deep commitment to fostering international understanding and peace, as evidenced by professional and academic accomplishments, as well as personal or community service
  • For Master’s degree applicants: a minimum of three years of relevant full-time work or volunteer experience, in addition to holding a bachelor’s degree
  • For Certificate applicants: a minimum of five years of relevant full-time work or volunteer experience, coupled with a solid academic background

How do I apply for Rotary Peace Fellowships?

To apply for Rotary Peace Fellowships, the following instructions must be followed:

  1. Familiarize yourself with the application guidelines and review the eligibility requirements and restrictions.
  2. Explore the curriculum and programs offered at each of the Rotary Peace Centers. You will be asked to rank your two preferred centers.
  3. Prepare your application, which should include your resume or CV, essays, academic or professional recommendations, transcripts, and test scores. Please note that all materials must be in English. For certificate candidates, transcripts and scores are not required.
  4. Prior to submitting your application, reach out to your local Rotary district to request an interview. District endorsement is a mandatory part of the application process. TIP: Your local Rotary club can assist you in connecting with your district. Use the Club Finder to locate the nearest club. While club interviews and recommendations are strongly encouraged, candidates with district support are not obliged to have them.
  5. Submit your online application to your district by May 15, before any interviews take place.
  6. Engage in an interview with district representatives. Districts must submit endorsements to the Rotary Foundation by July 1.
  7. Await notification of the selection results, which will be announced in November. If you are chosen for a fellowship, you will be informed of the Peace Center where you will pursue your studies.
  8. Apply for admission to the university affiliated with your Peace Center. It is important to note that being selected for the fellowship does not guarantee admission to the university.

Application Steps:

Let’s get started with the steps you need to take in order to apply for this prestigious fellowship.

Step 1: Review Eligibility Criteria

Before submitting your application, please review the eligibility criteria on our Rotary website to ensure that you meet all the requirements. This includes having a proven track record of working on peace and conflict resolution initiatives, as well as being fluent in English.

Step 2: Prepare Required Documents

In order to complete your application, you will need to gather several documents including a resume/CV, letters of recommendation, and academic transcripts. Be sure to have these ready before starting the application process.

Step 3: Complete Application Form

The application form can be found on our website and is typically available during the application period. The form will ask for personal information, educational background, work experience, and details about your peacebuilding initiatives.

Step 4: Write Personal Statement

One of the most important parts of your application is the personal statement. This is your opportunity to showcase your passion for peacebuilding and why you are a strong candidate for the program. Be sure to highlight any relevant experience, skills, and goals that align with our mission.

Step 5: Submit Required Documents

Once you have completed the application form and written your personal statement, it’s time to submit all required documents. This may include a resume/CV, letters of recommendation, academic transcripts, and any other supporting materials.

Step 6: Application Review

After submitting your application, our team will review it thoroughly. We take into consideration all aspects of the application including personal statements, academic records, work experience, and recommendations.

Step 7: Interview Process

If selected as a potential candidate, you will be invited for an interview with our team. This is an opportunity for us to get to know you better and for you to learn more about the organization and the position.

Step 8: Decision

After the interview process, our team will carefully consider all candidates and make a final decision. If selected, we will reach out to you with a formal offer and further instructions on next steps.

What to Do After Submitting Application

After submitting your application, please check your email regularly for updates and notifications from our team. We may request additional information or documents from you during the review process.

Additionally, we encourage you to continue pursuing other opportunities and keeping an open mind about different paths. Our organization values growth and development, so even if this particular position does not work out, we hope that you will still consider us for future opportunities or keep in touch with our team.
