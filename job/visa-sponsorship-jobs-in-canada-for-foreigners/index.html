Visa Sponsorship Opportunities in Canada for Foreign Workers

What Does Visa Sponsorship Mean?

Visa sponsorship takes place when an individual, organization, or entity advocates for an individual’s visa.

When you apply for a visa, it signifies that you have a sponsor who supports your entry into the United States for the specified purposes. This sponsorship can extend to permanent visas, such as green cards, allowing for immigration to the country.

How to get Sponsorship to Work in Canada

Before applying for a work permit in Canada, it’s typically a requirement to have a job offer from a Canadian employer. The employer may need to go through the Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) process, which is managed by Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC). Here’s how the process generally works:

  1. Employer’s Application for LMIA:
    • The Canadian employer who wishes to hire a foreign worker applies for an LMIA to ESDC. They must demonstrate that they have made reasonable efforts to hire Canadian citizens or permanent residents for the job but were unsuccessful.
  2. LMIA Assessment:
    • ESDC reviews the employer’s application to assess the impact of hiring a foreign worker on the Canadian labor market. They consider factors such as the availability of Canadians for the position, the job’s wages, working conditions, and the potential benefits to the Canadian economy.
  3. Positive LMIA Result:
    • If ESDC approves the LMIA application, it means they have determined that hiring a foreign worker for the job will have a positive or neutral impact on the Canadian labor market. The employer receives a positive LMIA.
  4. Job Offer and Work Permit Application:
    • With a positive LMIA in hand, the Canadian employer can extend a formal job offer to the foreign worker. The worker can then apply for a work permit using the LMIA and job offer as supporting documents.
  5. Work Permit Application Processing:
    • The worker submits the work permit application, including all required documentation and fees, to the appropriate Canadian immigration authorities.
  6. Travel and Employment:
    • If the work permit is approved, the worker can travel to Canada and begin employment with the Canadian employer.

Work Permits

A Work Permit is a document or card that grants you permission to work in Canada. After finding sponsorship employment in Canada that will grant you an LMIA, you must get a work permit. Work permits are classified into two types:

Open Work Permit

There are no restrictions on this sort of work visa. The work permit provides no information regarding the company’s employment. It does not even need a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) or an employment offer.

Obtaining a work visa in Canada does not have to be difficult, but you must satisfy the following criteria:

  • Are the spouse or common-law partner of a temporary foreign worker in vocations designated by the National Occupation Classification (NOC) as Level O, A, or B;
  • Are you the spouse or common-law partner of an international student studying full-time?
  • Are an international student who has just graduated from a Canadian post-secondary institution.

Employer-Specific Work Permit

There are several restrictions with this sort of work visa. It describes the nature of work and the name of your employer, limiting you to that position.

If you want to work for a different employer, you must apply for a new work permit; but, in most cases, you will keep your present work permit.

You must have a job offer and a Labour Market Impact Assessment to get this type of work visa (LMIA). Once you’ve satisfied these two requirements, you can apply for a work permit.
