Cornell University Admissions

Cornell University has a highly competitive admissions process. Admissions decisions are based on a holistic review process that takes into account many factors beyond just GPA and test scores.

The application requirements for Cornell University include the Common Application, school report and counselor recommendation, teacher evaluations, standardized test scores (either SAT or ACT), and TOEFL or IELTS scores for international applicants. In addition, applicants are required to submit a writing supplement that includes specific essays or short answer questions related to their intended major or program.

Cornell University offers both early decision and regular decision options for applicants. Early decision is binding, meaning that if an applicant is accepted, they must attend Cornell and withdraw all other applications. The regular decision application deadline is typically in early January, and admissions decisions for both early decision and regular decision are typically released in March.

Cornell University Acceptance Rate

Cornell University is a highly selective institution, and its acceptance rate varies by year and program. According to data from the 2023 admissions cycle, Cornell’s overall acceptance rate was 11.2%. The acceptance rate for Cornell’s individual colleges and schools ranged from 4.2% to 24.1%, with some programs being even more selective.

Cornell Early Decision Acceptance Rate

The early decision acceptance rate for Cornell University varies by year and program. According to data from the 2023 admissions cycle, Cornell’s early decision acceptance rate was 23.8%. Hhow to Get into Cornell University

Getting into Cornell University is a highly competitive process. However, here are some tips that may increase your chances:
  1. Maintain a high GPA: Cornell considers a strong academic record as one of the most important factors in their admissions process.
  2. Score well on standardized tests: High SAT or ACT scores can enhance your application, but keep in mind that standardized tests are just one of many factors that Cornell considers.
  3. Demonstrate extracurricular achievements: Admissions officers look for well-rounded students who have excelled in extracurricular activities, such as sports, music, community service, or research.
  4. Write a compelling essay: Your essay is an opportunity to showcase your personality, interests, and writing skills. Make sure to proofread and have someone else review your essay as well.
  5. Get strong letters of recommendation: Letters of recommendation from teachers or other mentors who know you well and can attest to your strengths can help bolster your application.
  6. Apply early: Applying early decision or early action can increase your chances of being admitted, but keep in mind that these programs are binding and require you to commit to attending if accepted.
