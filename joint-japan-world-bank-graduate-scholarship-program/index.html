Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program 2024

The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program is currently accepting applications, offering an incredible opportunity to pursue higher education at renowned universities across the United States, Europe, Africa, Oceania, and Japan. In this article, we will provide comprehensive information about this prestigious scholarship program, its advantages, and a step-by-step guide to the application process. If you’re eager to further your education and seek financial support for your studies, read on to discover more about this exceptional program.

The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program for the academic years 2024–2026 is a fully funded scholarship opportunity designed for international students aspiring to pursue master’s degree programs. This scholarship covers various essential aspects of your education, including full tuition fees, accommodation, economy-class air travel, and a monthly living stipend. It provides comprehensive financial support to help you focus on your studies and achieve your academic goals. If you’re searching for financial assistance to pursue your master’s degree, the JJ/WBGSP scholarship program is definitely worth considering.

The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program (JJ/WBGSP) is an inclusive scholarship opportunity available to both women and men from developing nations. To qualify for this scholarship, applicants should have relevant professional experience and a proven track record of contributing to the development efforts of their respective countries. This scholarship is specifically designed for those applying to a master’s degree program in a field related to development. Its aim is to empower individuals to pursue higher education in areas that can contribute to the sustainable development of their home countries.

The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program (JJ/WBGSP) provides scholarships for 26 participating programs at universities located in the United States, Europe, Africa, Oceania, and Japan. These programs cover various crucial areas of development, including economic policy management, tax policy, and infrastructure management. To be eligible for a JJ/WBGSP scholarship, it is essential to gain admission to one of these participating programs. Applicants from developing nations who receive this scholarship are committed to returning to their home countries after graduation to apply their newly acquired skills and contribute to the cultural and economic development of their nations. This commitment reflects the program’s goal of fostering sustainable development and capacity-building in developing nations.

Scholarship Summary

  • Level of Study: Masters
  • Institution(s): Newcastle University
  • Study in the U.S., Europe, Africa, Oceania and Japan
  • Opportunity Focus Areas: Click here to find the available masters opportunities
  • Program Period: 2 years
  • Deadline: (The next windows for scholarship applications will open in January and March 2024.)
    • Application Window 1: From January 16 to February 28, 2024, at noon Washington time
    • Application Window 2: From March 27 to May 26, 2024, at noon Washington time.

Scholarship Coverage

The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program provides the recipient with the following benefits:

  • Economy class air travel between your home country and the host university at the start of your study program and immediately following the end of the scholarship period In addition to the two-way air travel, scholars will receive a US $500 travel allowance for each trip;
  • Tuition for your graduate program and the cost of basic medical insurance obtained through the university.
  • While on campus during the scholarship period, a monthly subsistence allowance is provided to cover all living expenses (accommodations, food, etc., including books). The amount of the allowance varies depending on the host country.

Please note that all JJ/WBGSP scholarships cover the duration of the graduate program or two years, whichever is less.

The JJ/WBGSP scholarship does not cover:

  • Costs associated with VISA application(s)
  • Expenses for supporting and/or bringing family members of a scholar
  • Extra-curricular courses or training
  • Courses that do not meet the minimum credit requirements for earning a graduate degree
  • Language training not provided by the graduate program
  • Additional travel expenses during the study program
  • Expenses related to research, supplementary educational materials, field trips, workshops or seminars, and internships
  • Educational equipment, such as computers
  • Resident permit fee

Eligibility Criteria for Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program

To be eligible for The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program – JJWBGSP, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Required Languages: English
  • Eligible Countries: All countries worldwide
  • Must not possess dual citizenship of any developed country
  • Must be in good health
  • Must hold a Bachelor’s (or equivalent) degree earned at least 3 years prior to the Application Deadline date
  • Must have a minimum of 3 years of recent work experience in a development-related field after earning a Bachelor’s (or equivalent) degree
  • Must be currently employed in a paid full-time position in a development-related field at the time of submitting the scholarship application. The only exception to this criterion is for nationals of developing countries listed as Fragile and Conflict States in the updated list provided to applicants in the Application Guidelines for each scholarship call
  • Must have an unconditional admission (except for funding) to at least one of the preferred master’s programs at a JJ/WBGSP partner university located outside the applicant’s country of citizenship and country of residence. The letter of admission must be uploaded prior to submitting the application
  • Must not be an Executive Director, alternate, or staff member of the World Bank Group, or a close relative of any of the aforementioned individuals. “Close relative” refers to Mother, Father, Sister, Half-sister, Brother, Half-brother, Son, Daughter, Aunt, Uncle, Niece, or Nephew. Please note that all eligibility criteria are strictly adhered to and no exceptions will be made. The eligibility criteria will remain unchanged throughout the application process, although this information is subject to change between the closure of one application process and the opening of the next.

How to Apply for The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program?

Please follow the following steps to avail this scholarship:

  • To begin, you must apply for admission to one or more of the [Partner Masters Degree Program(s)](https://www.worldbank.org/en/programs/scholarships/brief/jjwbgsp-participating-programs). If you have any inquiries regarding how to submit an admissions application to a partner program, please direct them to the respective university.
